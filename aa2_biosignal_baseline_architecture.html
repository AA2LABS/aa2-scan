WHERE VIDEO GOES
Facial + expression analysis
Video is analyzed for micro-expressions, skin tone shifts, eye movement patterns, and smile/frown/cry/laugh signals. A smile during a "stressful" Javier question recontextualizes the Garmin HRV reading — Bio Buddy may stay silent even if hardware shows stress. Stored as anchor point in biosignal_baselines.
WHERE VOICE GOES
Acoustic baseline layer
Voice recordings are analyzed for tone, pace, pitch, tremor, and breath pattern. Words matter less than delivery. Hesitation and confidence are audible before the conscious mind has language for them. Stored in biosignal_baselines as stimulus_type + input_tier = VOICE.
WHERE BYOB FILES GO
biosignal_history table
Garmin exports, Oura CSVs, Apple Health XMLs upload through Q20. Raw RR interval data passes through Kubios API for clinical HRV interpretation. RMSSD, SDNN, LF/HF ratio, Stress Index, Recovery score — all stored. This is what makes the baseline clinical-grade, not estimated.
WHERE TEXT GOES
Lowest accuracy — word choice only
Text answers to Javier's questions are the floor tier. Word choice and vocabulary are captured. Emotionally complex members with limited vocabulary will under-report — which is exactly why video and voice exist. Text alone never builds a complete baseline.
Three input tiers — accuracy descends
Canon doctrine: all four layers (hardware + video + voice + text) triangulate simultaneously. The membrane forms its own interpretation. The devices don't get the last word. The membrane does.

Pattern recognition active. Bio Buddy begins speaking more precisely. The Chef adjusts to actual metabolic response, not just stated goals.

BEYOND 90 · KNOWING

Supabase tables — where every input lands
All writes go through lib/db.ts — the sovereignty layer. The app never calls Supabase directly.
biosignal_baselines
Stimulus anchor points from Javier's emotional questions. Fields: member_id · stimulus_type (POSITIVE/STRESS/NEUTRAL) · input_tier (VIDEO/VOICE/TEXT) · rmssd_response · sdnn_response · stress_index · anchor_confirmed · created_at
Video · Voice · Kubios
biosignal_history
Raw biosignal uploads from Q20 BYOB. Garmin exports, Oura CSVs, Apple Health XMLs, Google Fit data, voice memos, video uploads, or optional text notes. Field: uploaded_file (path or note)
BYOB / BYOH files
baseline_profiles
Subjective baseline from Q15 (sleep_score 1–5) and Q16 (stress_level: Low/Moderate/High/Unpredictable). These are the member's self-reported starting point before hardware data refines them.
Q15 · Q16 text answers
allergy_profiles
Food allergens · suspected sensitivities · personal care allergens · environmental triggers. Flagged first in every scan. Permanent. Never expires.
Q7–Q10 chips
health_profiles
Conditions · active_limits (permanent scan filter) · medications · diet_types. Active limits run silently on every scan forever.
Q11–Q14 chips
member_profiles
name · age · species_protected · delivery_mode (VIDEO/VOICE/TEXT) · color_mode · onboarding_complete. The delivery_mode here is what the member chose in Screen 1 — how Javier speaks to them.
Screen 1 + Q1–Q4
goal_profiles
primary_goal array · vision_text. These feed buildPersonalTruth() so every scan verdict references what the member said they're trying to achieve.
Q5–Q6 text
device_connections
hardware array (Garmin, Oura, Apple Watch, etc). Determines which BYOH devices are active and routing data through Kubios.
Q18 BYOH chips
buildPersonalTruth(profile) — the output of all 8 tables
When a member scans, loadMemberProfile() reads all 8 tables. The assembled profile passes into buildPersonalTruth() which prepends context to every system prompt. The Equalizer now knows everything. Personal truth fires. Generic truth is gone.
